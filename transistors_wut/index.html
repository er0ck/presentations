diode

- anyone know what n-type or p-type means? - seriously, there will be a quiz on this later - what happens at the junction in the middle? - what happens when you apply a voltage (bias)? - arrow is direction of conventional current

diode

- at "zero bias" when materials are combined, a depletion region forms - a region where the carriers physically diffuse across the boundary due to lower concentrations - some current flows, but it's small, solely due to "drift" - at "forward bias" the depletion region shrinks and we acheive a stronger "diffusion current" - at "negative bias" what happens? - the depletion region grows. so? - the diffusion current is almost zero and we have a small reverse "saturation current" - more on these later...

1951

FET/MOS


idea patented in 1925, first manufactured in 1959 @ bell labs
- Field Effect Transistor - Metal Oxide Semiconductor - can be used as a switch - also useful as amplifier/modulator - less power consumption - ironically invented at bell labs, but deemed unattractive for manufacturing - now the definition of precise manufacturing, down to 10nm

- "complementary" MOS - high noise immunity - low standby (non-switching) power use - very low current flow when not switching

Logic Synthesis
